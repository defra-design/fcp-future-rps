Version 1 Design history

Why we explored the concept of an 'Action Centred Agreement'

Problem statement

The Rural Payments Agency (RPA) and farmers/agents currently manage six live land-based schemes in the existing Rural Payments service.

All these schemes use actions as the common unit of work a farmer can sign up to do.

Currently each scheme has it's own set of rules, operating model and terms and conditions. This has introduced complexity to the funding offer and made the service rigid and inflexible.

This internal organisation makes it hard to manage change for:

  • external users who need to adapt what they're doing on their land to respond to evolving circumstances
  • operations staff who need to implement changes to policy or payment rates and carry out annual scheme transitions
  • policy makers who need to adjust the funding offer if itâ€™s not delivering the intended environmental outcomes

The lack of flexibility and inability to handle changes results in:>

  • external users being fearful that making a change will be complex and may put payments at risk or cause delays. This results in them:
  • limiting themselves to sign up for actions they feel are easy to deliver even if they're ineffective
  • not changing to actions that may be beneficial to their new conditions or the environment
  • operational staff adopting inefficient processes adding to their administrative burden
  • policy makers struggling to meet environmental targets due to reduced uptake in actions that deliver the desired outcomes

Alpha definition

Therefore, we want to create one service for farmers to be able to apply for and manage land-based grants. This will take an action-centred approach which has three interdependent strands:

  1. Simplified/single/stable funding offer.
  2. Flexible funding agreement/s.
  3. Improved system capability.

These strands contribute in the following ways:

1. Simplified stable funding offer

A simplified stable offer:

  • where existing actions have been rationalised and ratified
  • that moves away from 'big-bang' annual scheme roll out
  • where change is introduced and managed at action level
  • that is easy to understand and accessible to all
  • with actions as the fundamental core of the offer
  • where revenue and capital options are managed within one offer
  • where environmental outputs are visible, demonstrable and drive ambition

2. Flexible funding agreements

An agreement is the legally viable document that provides the legal framework required to deliver the funding.

The service approach to agreements will allow users to manage their agreements in a way that meets their needs. This includes:

  • using single or multiple agreements
  • structured to accommodate different types of land tenure
  • managing both simple and complex actions and all ambition levels in a single agreement
  • structured to reduce complexity at all stages (such as payments, declarations)
  • the ability for customers to add/remove/amend actions or land (with appropriate controls) to respond to change or increase ambition level

3. Improved system capability

A consistent action definition (framework) that provides the necessary data architecture for the system to:

  • make it easier for farmers and land managers to manage the actions on their land
  • measure take up of each action or capital item
  • mesure and track the impact of actions on environmental outcomes
  • enable effective reporting
  • direct tasks through to appropriate operational teams (incl. ALBs)
  • complete compatibility and eligibility checks
  • enable change to be implemented effectively
  • provide sufficient levers/mechanisms to target/manage funding commitment against budgets and deliverability

User needs

Full set of user needs found here.

  1. "As a Farmer or land manager in an agri-environment scheme, I need to be clear what funding is available for my land and the way I work it."
  2. "As a Farmer or land manager in an agri-environment scheme, I need to be able to apply for additional funding, to bring in more land, undertake more actions or buy more items, while already in an agri-environment scheme so that I can do more than originally planned and respond to changing circumstances."
  3. "As a Farmer or land manager in an agri-environment scheme, I need to be able to amend what I've signed up for, so I can adapt to changes happening on my farm."
  4. "As a Farmer or land manager in an agri-environment scheme, I need the impact of making changes to be clear so I can make an informed decision."
